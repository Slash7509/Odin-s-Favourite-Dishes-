Odin's Feast: Valhalla Stew

Ingredients

  • 1 lb venison (cubed)
    •
  • 1 lb boar meat (cubed)
  • 2 large potatoes (diced)
  • 3 large carrots (sliced)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup barley
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups mead
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or rendered animal fat
  • 1 cup mushrooms (sliced)
    •

Instructions

  1. Preparation:

    • In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil or rendered animal fat over medium-high heat.
    • Add the cubed venison and boar meat. Sear until browned on all sides. Remove the meat and set aside.

  2. Sautéing:

    • In the same pot, add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
    • Add the minced garlic and sauté for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Building the Stew:

    • Return the seared meats to the pot.
    • Add the diced potatoes, sliced carrots, and sliced mushrooms. Stir to combine.
    • Pour in the beef broth, mead, and water. Stir to mix all ingredients.

  4. Seasoning:

    • Add the barley, bay leaves, rosemary, and thyme.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Simmering:

    • Bring the stew to a boil.
    • Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the barley is cooked through. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  6. Final Touches:

    • Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
    • Remove the bay leaves, rosemary, and thyme sprigs before serving.

  7. Serving

    • Serve the Valhalla Stew hot, ideally in rustic bowls, and garnish with fresh herbs if desired.
    • Pair with a hearty loaf of bread and a goblet of mead for an authentic Viking experience.

Notes:

  • Historical Touch: Odin, as the Allfather in Norse mythology, would appreciate a hearty, robust meal that embodies the strength and endurance of a warrior.

  • Adaptation:Adaptation: Feel free to add other root vegetables like parsnips or turnips, or swap the meats for what you have available, to make this recipe your own.
